Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made with milk solids, sugar, and rose water. It is a soft, spongy, and sweet treat that is often served with a cup of tea or coffee.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour

  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder

  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder

  • 1/2 cup ghee

  • 2 cups sugar

  • 1/2 cup water

  • 1 teaspoon rose water

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the milk powder, flour, baking powder, and cardamom powder. Mix well.

  2. Add the ghee and mix until the mixture comes together to form a dough.

  3. Shape the dough into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter.

  4. Heat the oil in a deep saucepan over medium heat.

  5. Fry the gulab jamuns in batches until golden brown, about 3-5 minutes per batch.

  6. Remove the gulab jamuns from the oil and drain on paper towels.

  7. In a separate saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat.

  8. Reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes, or until the sugar dissolves.

  9. Add the rose water and stir to combine.

  10. Add the gulab jamuns to the syrup and simmer for 10 minutes, or until they are soaked through.

  11. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips:

  • For a richer flavor, use whole milk powder instead of skim milk powder. If you don't have baking powder, you can use 1/4 teaspoon baking soda instead.

  • To make the gulab jamuns even more flavorful, you can add a few drops of vanilla extract to the dough.

  • You can also add other flavors to the syrup, such as saffron or cardamom.
