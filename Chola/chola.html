Chole Bhature

It is a combination of chana masala and bhatura/puri, a deep-fried bread made from maida. Chole bhature is often eaten as a breakfast dish, sometimes accompanied with lassi.

Ingredients

  • 2 cup soaked overnight chickpeas

  • 2 chopped onion

  • 1/4 cup tomato puree

  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder

  • 5 cloves garlic

  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric

  • 1 1/2 teaspoon chana masala powder

  • 1 teaspoon cumin

  • 1 tablespoon ginger

  • 3 tablespoon onion paste

  • salt as required

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder

  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder

  • 1 cup all purpose flour

  • 3 teaspoon yoghurt (curd)

  • refined oil as required

  • 1/4 tablespoon wheat flour

  • 2 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. To prepare the chole, soak chickpeas in water overnight or for 6-8 hours.

  2. Then, put a pressure cooker on high flame and add the soaked chickpeas in it along with salt to taste and 1 tea bag or you can also put 2-3 tea leaves and wrap it in a cloth. Cook the chickpeas for 15-20 minutes. (Note: The tea leaves or the tea bag will change the colour of gravy from dark brown to black.)

  3. Meanwhile, put a pan on medium flame and heat a little oil in it.

  4. Add the cumin seeds and sliced onion to the pan.

  5. Saute the onion till golden brown, then add minced garlic, ginger and onion paste.

  6. Saute the ingredients for a few seconds until the raw smell of garlic goes away and then add tomato puree.

  7. Once the oil is separated, add turmeric, red chilli powder, coriander powder and chana masala.

  8. Cook the tomato-onion mixture for another two minutes, and then add cooked chole with little water.

  9. Stir well to mix the masala with the chole or the chickpeas.

  10. Check salt and allow it to cook for a few minutes. The chola is done! You can garnish with chopped onion and coriander leaves.

  11. Now for making the bhaturas, combine together all-purpose flour or maida and wheat flour or atta in a dough kneading plate.

  12. Then add salt and oil to it. Mix well. Add yoghurt to the flour mixture and knead the dough well. You can add a little water and knead the dough.

  13. When it is done, cover the dough with a kitchen towel and allow it to rise.

  14. Now, put a kadhai on high flame and meanwhile, take equal quantities of the dough and roll big size puris (bhatura).

  15. When the oil is hot enough, carefully add these bhaturas to the oil and deep fry.

  16. Repeat with the rest of the dough. Then take a plate and serve Chola bhatura hot!

Tips:

  • To make the bhatura soft you can add some ghee, this will make your bhatura delicious.

  • You can add some amchur powder to the chole, to give it a spicy-tangy touch.

  • To make the dish more enticing you can dry roast the spices and then add them to the dish.

  • Add a bit of soda to give a nice texture to the Bhature and to make them puff up perfectly, make sure that the oil is super-hot.
