Butter Chicken

Butter chicken, traditionally known as murgh makhani, is an Indian dish originating in Delhi. It is a type of curry made from chicken with a spiced tomato and butter (makhan) sauce. Its sauce is known for its rich texture. It is similar to chicken tikka masala, which uses a tomato paste.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound skinless chicken breasts

  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided 1 onion, chopped

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder

  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala

  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce

  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar

  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste

  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, toss the chicken with the cornstarch, salt, and pepper.

  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

  3. Add the chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5 minutes. Transfer the chicken to a plate.

  4. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the skillet. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute more, until fragrant.

  5. Stir in the red chili powder, garam masala, and turmeric powder. Cook for 1 minute more, until fragrant.

  6. Stir in the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and tomato paste. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes, until the sauce is thickened.

  7. Serve the butter chicken

Tips:

  • For a spicier butter chicken, add more red chili powder to taste. If you don't have rice vinegar, you can use white vinegar instead.

  • You can also add other vegetables to the butter chicken, such as carrots or celery.

  • Serve with a side of naan bread for dipping.
