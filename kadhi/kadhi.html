Kadhi

Kadhi is a popular dish mainly consumed in South Asia. It consists of a thick gravy or soup based on gram flour.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup buttermilk

  2. 1/2 cup gram flour

  3. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder

  4. 1 teaspoon ground coriander

  5. 1 teaspoon ground cumin

  6. 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek powder .

  7. 1/2 teaspoon chili powder

  8. 1/2 teaspoon salt

  9. 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

  10. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

  11. 1/2 teaspoon curry leaves

  12. 1/4 teaspoon hing (asafoetida)

  13. 1/4 cup chopped cilantro

  14. 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds

  15. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  • In a bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, gram flour, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, fenugreek powder, chili powder, salt, and black pepper.

  • Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.

  • Add the mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, and hing. Cook for 30 seconds, or until the mustard seeds start to pop.

  • Add the tadka to the buttermilk mixture and whisk until smooth.

  • Bring the kadhi to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes, or until thickened.

  • Serve hot with rice or roti.

Tips:

  • Use fresh buttermilk for the best flavor.

  • If you don't have buttermilk, you can make your own by adding lemon juice or vinegar to regular milk.

  • Be careful not to overcook the kadhi, or it will become too thick.

  • If you want a thicker kadhi, you can add more gram flour.

  • You can also add other vegetables to kadhi, such as potatoes, cauliflower, or spinach.
