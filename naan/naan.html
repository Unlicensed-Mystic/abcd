Naan

Naan is a leavened flatbread mostly cooked in a tandoor. It is one of the most ordered flatbreads in Indian restaurants and is eaten with a curry or dal(lentils).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all purpose flour

  • 1 tablespoon butter

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1/2 cup water

  • 1/2 tablespoon refined oil

  • 1/4 cup yoghurt (curd)

  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda

  • milk as required

Instructions

  1. To prepare this easy naan recipe, sieve together the dry ingredients into a kneading plate.

  2. Once done, add butter, curd, oil in the dry ingredients and crumble using your fingers.

  3. Add little milk (as and when required) in the mixture, till the dough is soft.

  4. Now cover the dough with wet muslin cloth. Place another plate over it and keep aside for 5 - 6 hours. Once done, knead the dough lightly. Then take portions of the dough and make small balls of it.

  5. Roll the dough ball into a thick triangle. You may also use dry flour if required.

  6. Now to prepare the naan, heat a tawa over medium flame.

  7. Now using hands, apply little water on the top part of the rolled triangle and place the naan on the tawa.

  8. Now carefully, invert the griddle and roast the naan over the gas flame direct. Once done, transfer it to a serving plate and serve hot with a dollop of butter on it.

  9. You can pair it with gravy dishes like Dal Makhani or even Butter Chicken.

Tips:

  • For making that perfect naan at home, make sure the flour you use has high gluten-content in it. The gluten will ensure the elasticity and chewiness of the naan.

  • For making it fluffy and the restaurant-way, make sure you are using the right raising agent, i.e., either baking powder or yeast to make the dough.

  • The dough is the key to this recipe. For making those delicious naans, your dough should be soft, stretchy and sticky. If your dough has dried, add a little water or milk to it to make it perfect.
